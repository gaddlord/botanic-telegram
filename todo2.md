2. Love
The yellow and light-green sections of the wheel correlate to the complex Victorian "Language of Flowers," which often distinguished between "Enchanted," "Sentimental," and "Compassionate" love.

Honeysuckle: Generous and devoted affection.


Yellow Acacia: Secret love.

Gillyflower: Bonds of affection.

Heliotrope: Devotion and faithfulness.

3. Sadness
The blue sector of your wheel includes "Grief," "Lonely," and "Disappointed," which the Victorians frequently communicated through darker floral symbols.

Aloe: Grief and religious superstition.

Purple Hyacinth: Sorrow; "I am sorry".

Marigold: Grief and cruelty.

Asphodel: Regrets following one to the grave.

Zephyr Flower (Anemone): Forsaken or sickness.

4. Surprise
This correlates to the teal section of the wheel, representing being "Moved," "Amazed," or "Stunned."

Fern: Fascination and magic.

Arum (Wake Robin): Ardor (often associated with sudden excitement or being "overcome").

China Aster: Afterthought (reflecting the "Confused" or "Perplexed" sub-emotions).

5. Fear
The orange sector of the wheel includes "Anxious," "Insecure," and "Terrified." In floriography, these were often warnings or signs of internal distress.

Lavender: Distrust.

Dahlia: Instability.

Common Almond: Indiscretion (often leading to the sub-emotion of "Mortified").

Amaryllis: Timidity (correlating to "Insecure").

6. Anger
The purple and red-violet sections of the wheel focus on "Hostile," "Disgusted," and "Jealous."

Yellow Rose: Jealousy.

Hydrangea: Heartlessness.

Yellow Carnation: Disdain.

Buttercup: Ingratitude.
